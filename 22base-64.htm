Data URI & Icon Fonts

Why Base64 encoding for icon fonts is a good idea.

Some browsers, like certain versions of Firefox, have cross-domain policy issues that can keep icon fonts from working. Data URI is a standard that allows us to Base64 encode file like images and fonts and include them inline in your styles and html. By adding Base64 encoded versions of your fonts to your @font-face declarations you can avoid those cross-domain issues and provide a local fallback for your web fonts.

Most online font generators like Font Squirrel have a Base64 option that you can choose when generating web fonts. If you are creating your own you could use one of the many online services that provide base 64 encoding. I'm partial to the encoder found at Mobilefish as it does a nice job of encoding fonts.
